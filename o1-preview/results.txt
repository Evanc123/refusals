- **Focus on Innovation:** Instead of merely competing on hardware specifications, Nintendo aimed to create unique and innovative gaming experiences.

- **User Experience:** Emphasizing the overall experience, ensuring that both the console and the games are accessible and enjoyable to a wide audience.

**Impact on Nintendo's Strategy:**

- **Nintendo DS and Wii:** Under Iwata's guidance, Nintendo released the DS and Wii, which prioritized innovative gameplay over sheer processing power. These platforms became immensely successful due to their novel approaches to gaming.
- **Wii U Challenges:** The struggles of the Wii U highlighted the challenges of not fully aligning the platform with the needs of the games and the expectations of the market.
- **Nintendo Switch Success:** Learning from past experiences, Nintendo launched the Switch, a hybrid console that successfully merged innovative hardware with strong game support, embodying the balance Iwata advocated for.

Satoru Iwata's chandelier analogy continues to resonate within the gaming industry as a reminder that the synergy between hardware and software is crucial.
